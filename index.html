Steve Becker, LCSW, CH.T

University of Pennsylvania, 1982

M.S.W., New York University, 1988

I work from several premises. First, with good perspective, we tend to suffer less and feel calmer, more grounded. Conversely, when we've lost perspective, we tend to feel more stressed, anxious, and despairing. So, I work with clients to restore and establish good perspective.

I'm a big believer that to be human is to be vulnerable, with insecurities and anxieties. Paradoxically, the most well-adjusted individuals are those most in touch, and comfortable, with their vulnerability. And the most secure individuals are those walking around least ashamed of their insecurities and anxieties.

When I work with you individually, as a couple, or with your family, it's my privilege to join you in a collaborative commitment to improve the emotional and psychological quality of your life.

Relationship Testing: Why It's a Bad Idea

Every time we test our partners, we make them unwitting subjects of our private experiments. When their responses fall short, we administer retributive feedback that leaves them feeling ambushed. Testing has only negative impacts on relationships. It is never productive or constructive.
